  • Ind-Barath Power Gencom Limited (IBPGL)


    IBPGL is a 3 x 63 mw thermal power plant at Thoothukudi, Tamil Nadu. The main generating equipment consists of three steam turbine generator sets and three CFBC boilers manufactured by Hangzhou Steam Turbine Company Limited and Hangzhou Boiler Group Company Limited, respectively. Each turbine generator set is capable of producing power up to 63 MW. The use of newer CFBC technology allows to burn a wider range of fuel types. CFBC technology is considered to be more environmentally friendly compared to other conventional technologies and is also comparatively economical to maintain. IBPGL is also equipped with air-cooled condensers which result in efficient water usage.


    The estimated fuel requirement will be 66,750 metric tonnes per month at 100.0% PLF and an assumed gross calorific value of 5,400 kcal.


    Operational Energy Group India Private Limited (“OEG”) was appointed as the O&M contractor. The appointment of OEG is valid for a period of five years.


    IBPGL delivers generated power using 230 KV transmission line to TNEB Meelavittan sub-station.The plant area covers 73.21 acres of land and we also maintain industrial all risk policies for the plant.

    Ind-Barath Thermal Power Limited, Phase - I (IBTPL)


    IBTPL is a 2 x 150 mw thermal power plant at Thoothukudi, Tamil Nadu. The main generating equipment consists of two steam turbine generators and two CFBC boilers manufactured by Hangzhou Steam Turbine Company Limited and Hangzhou Boiler Group Company Limited, respectively. Each turbine generator set is capable of producing power upto 150 MW. The use of newer CFBC technology allows to burn a wider range of fuel types. CFBC technology is considered to be more environmentally friendly than certain conventional technologies and is also comparatively cheaper to maintain. The project is also equipped with air-cooled condensers which result in efficient water usage.


    IBTPL has signed a supply agreement with Greenesol Power Systems Private Limited. The scope of work under the supply agreement includes providing assistance in the design, engineering, manufacture/procurement of parts, packing and forwarding of parts and final handover of the constructed project. It also provides assistance in erection and providing technical training to employees.

  • Ind-Barath Energy (Utkal) Limited, Phase - I (IBEUL)


    IBEUL is currently in the process of implementing a 2 x 350 MW Coal-Based power project in the first phase at Jharsuguda District, Orissa, which is expected to be commissioned by August 2014.

    The main generating equipment of Phase I consists of two steam turbine generator sets and two pulverised coal fired reheat type boilers manufactured by Harbin Turbine Company Limited and Cethar Vessels Limited, respectively. Each turbine generator set is capable of producing power upto 350 MW.

    The estimated fuel requirement for Phase I will be 11,500 metric tonnes per day of domestic coal at 100.0% PLF and an assumed gross calorific value of 3,360 kcal.

    We are yet to enter into any O&M agreements for this project.

    IBEUL expects to deliver generated power using 400 KV transmission line to Jharsuguda sub-station.

    IBEUL Phase I area covers approximately 464 acres of land.

    Ind-Barath Power (Madras) Limited, Phase - I (IBPML)


    IBPML is currently implementing 660 MW Coal-Based power project in the first phase at Thoothukudi, Tamil Nadu, and is expected to be commissioned by June 2016.

    The main generating equipment of the project consists of one steam turbine generator and one PC boiler manufactured by Harbin Steam Turbine Company Limited and Harbin Boiler Company Limited, respectively. The turbine generator set is capable of producing power upto 660 MW of power. The project is also equipped with air-cooled condensers which result in efficient water usage.

    The estimated fuel requirement is 3.79 million metric tonnes per annum of Domestic Coal at 100.0% PLF and an assumed Gross Calorific Value of 3,360 kcal. We intend on procuring 70.0% of our coal requirement from Domestic Coal linkages and balance 30.0% from imported coal.

    IBPML, along with others, have signed a long term open access agreement with Power Grid Corporation of India Limited. Pursuant to this agreement, IBPML expects to deliver generated power from this project using 400 KV transmission line to Tuticorin Pooling Power Grid Corporation substation.

    The phase I area covers approximately 460 acres of land.
